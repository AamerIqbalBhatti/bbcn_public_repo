# BBCN — Locked reference numbers

Every number in the paper is generated by `python run_chunked.py` (full cohorts) and
emitted to `results/numbers/{numbers.tex, numbers.json, all_numbers.csv}`. The preprint
`\input`s `numbers.tex`, so the paper and the code cannot drift. This file is a
human-readable mirror of `all_numbers.csv`; the CSV is authoritative.

Two kernel-design methods are run and compared:
- **ranked** — the preserved reachability heuristic (impact → improvement → steps → causal).
  Reproduces the earlier BBCN118 kernels.
- **stabilize** — the canonical algebraic global-stabilisation test (Rafimanzelat,
  Theorem 1): clamp set accepted iff every column of Ω = L_c^ρ·T_S^z is the target index.
  Strictly stronger (drives *every* state to target, not just the patient's).

**Dynamic CDE controller, four-stage held-in-window** (% passing each stage)

| Cohort | Resistance | Apoptosis | Proliferation | Terminal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TCGA (1082)     | 14 \| 8 | 31 \| 27 | 65 \| 39 | 4 \| 3 |
| METABRIC (1980) | 7 \| 4  | 33 \| 29 | 61 \| 40 | 1 \| 1 |
| I-SPY2 (988)    | 13 \| 8 | 39 \| 35 | 60 \| 43 | 4 \| 3 |

Reading: ranked reproduces the prior numbers; on the static controller the two methods are
close; on the dynamic controller the algebraic method certifies fewer passes (largest gap on
proliferation), because it requires global stabilisation. The difference is the set of patients
the heuristic counts from a favourable start that are not globally stabilisable.
